Roasted Chicken with Clementines & Arak

by Yotam Ottolenghi — introduced to the family by Ginny, made a staple by André

All the intense flavours lavished on the chicken — arak, mustard, fennel, clementines with their skins, brown sugar — somehow manage to come together in a sweetly comforting dish you will always want to come back to. Serve with plainly cooked rice or bulgar.

Serves 4
Marinate A few hrs or overnight
Cook 35–45 min
Temp 220°C / 425°F

Ingredients

  • 100ml arak, ouzo, or Pernod
  • 4 tbsp olive oil
  • 3 tbsp freshly squeezed orange juice
  • 3 tbsp lemon juice
  • 2 tbsp grain mustard
  • 3 tbsp light brown sugar
  • 2½ tsp salt
  • 1½ tsp black pepper
  • 2 medium fennel bulbs, trimmed and cut into wedges
  • 1 large chicken (~1.3kg), cut into 8 pieces — or same weight in skin-on, bone-in thighs
  • 4 clementines, unpeeled, sliced horizontally into ½cm rounds
  • 1 tbsp thyme leaves
  • 2½ tsp fennel seeds, slightly crushed
  • Chopped flat-leaf parsley, to garnish

Instructions

  1. Combine the arak, olive oil, orange juice, lemon juice, grain mustard, and brown sugar in a large mixing bowl. Add 2½ tsp salt and 1½ tsp black pepper. Whisk well and set aside.
  2. Trim the fennel and cut each bulb in half lengthways. Cut each half into 4 wedges. Add the fennel, chicken pieces, clementine slices, thyme, and fennel seeds to the bowl. Mix everything well with your hands, then cover and marinate in the fridge for a few hours or overnight. (Skipping the marinade is fine if you're pressed for time.)
  3. Preheat the oven to 220°C / 425°F. Transfer the chicken and all the marinade to a large baking tray — roughly 30×37cm — so everything sits in a single layer. The chicken skin should be facing up.
  4. Roast for 35 to 45 minutes, until the chicken is deep golden and cooked through.
  5. Lift the chicken, fennel, and clementines from the tray and arrange on a serving plate. Cover loosely and keep warm. Pour the cooking juices into a small saucepan, bring to a boil, then simmer until reduced by a third — you want about 80ml of sauce. Pour the sauce over the chicken, scatter with chopped parsley, and serve.

Notes

Serve with plainly cooked rice or bulgar. The clementines go in unpeeled — the skin caramelizes and becomes part of the dish. If using chicken breasts instead of thighs, pull them out at around 30 minutes and add back for the last 5 to finish.
