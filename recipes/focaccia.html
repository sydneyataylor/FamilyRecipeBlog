Focaccia

Crisp on the bottom, pillowy inside, and pooled with good olive oil in every dimple. Rosemary and flaky salt on top — that's all it needs. Worth the patience.

Serves 8–10
Proof 2–3 hrs
Bake 25–30 min
Temp 450°F / 230°C

Ingredients

  • Dough
  • 500g (4 cups) bread flour or all-purpose flour
  • 7g (2¼ tsp) instant yeast
  • 10g (2 tsp) fine sea salt
  • 375ml (1½ cups) warm water (around 100°F / 38°C)
  • 60ml (¼ cup) good olive oil, plus more for the pan
  • Topping
  • 60ml (¼ cup) good olive oil
  • 4 sprigs fresh rosemary
  • Flaky sea salt (Maldon or similar)

Instructions

  1. In a large bowl, whisk together the flour, yeast, and salt. Add the warm water and olive oil. Mix with a wooden spoon or your hands until a shaggy dough comes together — it will be sticky. Cover the bowl and let it rest at room temperature for 1 hour.
  2. After 1 hour, perform 4 sets of stretch-and-folds over 45 minutes: every 15 minutes, wet your hand, grab one side of the dough, stretch it up as far as it will go, and fold it over the top. Rotate the bowl a quarter turn and repeat all the way around. The dough will become smoother and more elastic with each set.
  3. Generously coat a 9×13-inch (23×33cm) baking pan with olive oil — don't be shy, you want a pool of it. Transfer the dough to the pan and turn to coat in the oil. Gently stretch it toward the corners as best you can. If it springs back, let it rest 10 minutes and try again. Cover loosely and let proof for 45 minutes to 1 hour, until puffed and bubbly.
  4. Preheat the oven to 450°F / 230°C. Drizzle the remaining ¼ cup olive oil over the dough. With well-oiled fingers, dimple the entire surface aggressively — push all the way down to the bottom of the pan. The oil will pool in the dimples. Lay rosemary sprigs over the top and press gently. Scatter generously with flaky salt.
  5. Bake for 25 to 30 minutes, until the top is deep golden and the underside is crisp (lift a corner to check). Transfer to a wire rack and let cool for at least 10 minutes before cutting. Best eaten the day it's baked.

Notes

The overnight cold-proof version: after mixing, cover and refrigerate overnight (up to 18 hours) instead of proofing at room temperature. Take out 1 hour before baking, proceed with the pan transfer and dimpling. The cold proof develops more flavor. Add sliced olives, cherry tomatoes, or caramelized onions into the dimples before baking for a more dressed-up version.
