Tortilla Soup

by Rosewood Mansion on Turtle Creek

A deeply rich, restaurant-quality tortilla soup. The key is sautéing the tortillas directly in the pot — they dissolve into the broth and give it body. Garnish generously.

Serves 4
Prep 30 min
Cook 1 hr 30 min
Total 2 hrs

Ingredients

  • 3 tbsp corn oil
  • 4 corn tortillas, coarsely chopped
  • 6 cloves garlic, finely chopped
  • 1 tbsp chopped fresh epazote or cilantro
  • 1 cup fresh onion, pureed
  • 2 cups pureed fresh tomatoes
  • 1 tbsp ground cumin
  • 2 tsp chili powder
  • 2 whole bay leaves
  • 4 tbsp canned tomato puree
  • 2 quarts chicken stock
  • Salt and cayenne pepper to taste
  • For Garnish
  • 1 cooked chicken breast, cut into strips
  • 1 avocado, peeled, seeded, and cubed
  • 1 cup shredded Cheddar cheese
  • 3 corn tortillas, cut into strips and fried until crisp

Instructions

  1. Heat the corn oil in a large saucepan over medium heat. Sauté the chopped tortillas with the garlic and epazote until the tortillas are soft, about 5 minutes.
  2. Add the pureed onion and fresh tomato puree and bring to a boil. Add the cumin, chili powder, bay leaves, canned tomato puree, and chicken stock. Bring to a boil again, then reduce heat to a simmer.
  3. Season with salt and cayenne pepper to taste. Cook, stirring frequently, for 30 minutes. Skim any fat from the surface if needed.
  4. Strain the soup and pour into warm bowls. Garnish each bowl with equal portions of chicken, avocado, shredded cheese, and crispy tortilla strips. Serve immediately.
