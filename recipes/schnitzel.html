Schnitzel

Pounded thin, breaded just right, and fried until golden — the crust should shatter when you cut into it. Serve with a wedge of lemon and nothing else needs to happen.

Serves 4
Prep 20 min
Cook 15 min
Total ~35 min
photo coming soon

Ingredients

  • 4 boneless pork loin chops or veal cutlets (about 150g each)
  • Salt and freshly ground black pepper
  • ½ cup all-purpose flour
  • 2 large eggs
  • 1½ cups fine dry breadcrumbs
  • Neutral oil (canola or vegetable), for frying
  • 2 tbsp unsalted butter
  • Lemon wedges, to serve
  • Chopped flat-leaf parsley, to garnish

Instructions

  1. Place each cutlet between two sheets of plastic wrap or in a zip-lock bag. Using a meat mallet or heavy pan, pound to an even ¼-inch thickness. Season well on both sides with salt and pepper.
  2. Set up a breading station: flour in one shallow dish, eggs beaten in a second, breadcrumbs in a third. Working one at a time, dredge the cutlets in flour (shaking off excess), then egg (letting the excess drip off), then press firmly into breadcrumbs to coat evenly.
  3. Pour enough oil into a large skillet to come about ½ inch up the side. Heat over medium-high until shimmering. Add the butter. When the foam subsides, the fat is ready.
  4. Fry the schnitzels in batches — do not crowd the pan. Cook 2 to 3 minutes per side, until deep golden and crisp. Transfer to a wire rack or paper towel–lined plate.
  5. Serve immediately with lemon wedges and a scatter of parsley. The crust softens fast, so don't wait.

Notes

Pork is more forgiving than veal and just as delicious. Fine breadcrumbs give a more even crust than panko — avoid panko here. If the oil isn't hot enough the coating absorbs fat instead of crisping. Test with a pinch of breadcrumbs: it should sizzle immediately on contact.
