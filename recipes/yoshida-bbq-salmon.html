Yoshida BBQ Salmon

Yoshida's sweet-savory marinade does all the work here. A quick soak, then onto the grill or into the oven — it caramelizes beautifully and the salmon stays tender all the way through.

Serves 4
Marinate 30 min – overnight
Cook 12–15 min
Temp 400°F / 200°C

Ingredients

  • 4 salmon fillets (about 180g / 6 oz each), skin-on
  • 1 cup Yoshida's Original Gourmet Sauce
  • 1 tbsp soy sauce
  • 1 tsp sesame oil
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 tsp fresh ginger, grated (optional)
  • Sesame seeds, to garnish
  • Sliced green onions, to garnish

Instructions

  1. Combine the Yoshida sauce, soy sauce, sesame oil, garlic, and ginger in a shallow dish or zip-lock bag. Add the salmon fillets and turn to coat. Marinate in the refrigerator for at least 30 minutes, or up to overnight.
  2. Remove the salmon from the marinade and let any excess drip off. Reserve the marinade.
  3. To grill: Heat the grill to medium-high. Oil the grates well. Grill skin-side down for 4 to 5 minutes, then carefully flip and cook another 3 to 4 minutes, basting once with reserved marinade, until the fish flakes easily and the glaze is caramelized. To bake: Preheat to 400°F. Place fillets skin-side down on a foil-lined baking sheet. Bake 12 to 15 minutes, basting halfway through with reserved marinade, until cooked through.
  4. Transfer to a serving plate and garnish with sesame seeds and sliced green onions. Serve over steamed rice.

Notes

Don't skip the baste halfway through — that second coat is what gives the lacquered, sticky finish. The marinade can go in a small saucepan and be reduced to a thicker glaze for drizzling over at the end. Leftover salmon is excellent cold on top of salad greens the next day.
