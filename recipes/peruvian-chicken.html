Ginny's Peruvian Chicken

by Ginny

A family staple passed from Ginny to Anna to Steph to Sydney. The marinade does all the work overnight — the grill does the rest. Don't skip the green sauce.

Serves 4
Prep 30 min + overnight
Cook 1 hr 30 min
Total 2 hrs

Ingredients

  • For the Chicken
  • 3 tbsp extra virgin olive oil
  • ¼ cup lime juice (from 2 limes)
  • 4 large garlic cloves, roughly chopped
  • 1 tbsp kosher salt
  • 2 tsp paprika
  • 1 tsp black pepper
  • 1 tbsp cumin
  • 1 tsp dried oregano
  • 2 tsp sugar
  • 1 whole chicken (about 4 lbs)
  • For the Green Sauce
  • 3 jalapeño chili peppers, seeded if desired, roughly chopped
  • 1 cup fresh cilantro leaves
  • 2 cloves garlic, roughly chopped
  • ¾ cup fat-free sour cream
  • 1 tbsp fresh lime juice (from 1 lime)
  • ½ tsp salt
  • ⅛ tsp freshly ground black pepper
  • 2 tbsp extra virgin olive oil

Instructions

Marinate the chicken

  1. Combine the lime juice, olive oil, salt, pepper, sugar, paprika, cumin, and oregano in a blender. Blend until smooth.
  2. Using your fingers or a spoon, loosen the skin over the breasts and legs of the chicken. Spoon ⅔ of the marinade under the skin.
  3. Tie the legs together. Rub the remaining marinade over the outside. Place the chicken in a bowl, cover, and refrigerate overnight.

Make the green sauce

  1. Combine the sour cream, cilantro, jalapeños, garlic, lime juice, salt, and pepper in a blender or food processor. Process until smooth.
  2. With the motor running, drizzle in the olive oil. The sauce will seem thin at first but thickens as it sits. Refrigerate until ready to serve.

Grill

  1. Prepare the grill with one full chimney of charcoal. When the coals have a white edge, pour them onto one half of the grill.
  2. Grill the chicken over direct heat for 20–30 minutes, turning constantly to crisp up all sides.
  3. When the skin is golden and crispy, remove the chicken to a platter. Remove the grill grate and add about 20 new briquettes to the existing coals.
  4. Replace the grate and place the chicken breast-side up on the side with no coals. Cover with vents open — place the open vents directly above the chicken to pull in a smoky flavor. Cook for 45 minutes.
  5. Rest for 10 minutes before carving. Serve with the green sauce alongside.

Notes

This recipe was originally intended for oven roasting, but the family prefers it on the Weber Grill — the smoke makes all the difference. For the green sauce, heat comes from the ribs and seeds of the jalapeños. Start with half and blend in more to taste. Wash hands well after handling hot peppers.
