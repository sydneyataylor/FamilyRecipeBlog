Key Lime Pie

Three ingredients into a graham cracker crust, barely baked. Deceptively simple and the kind of pie that disappears before dinner is over. A summertime staple.

Serves 8
Prep 15 min
Cook 5–8 min
Total 1 hr 20 min
photo coming soon

Ingredients

  • 3 cups sweetened condensed milk
  • ¾ cup key lime juice
  • ½ cup sour cream
  • 1 tbsp grated lime zest
  • 1 (9-inch) prepared graham cracker crust
  • Whipped cream and lime slices, for serving

Instructions

  1. Preheat the oven to 350°F (175°C).
  2. Combine the condensed milk, key lime juice, sour cream, and lime zest in a bowl. Mix well. Pour into the graham cracker crust and place on a baking sheet.
  3. Bake for 5 to 8 minutes, until tiny pinhole bubbles burst on the surface of the pie. The filling will be wobbly but should not be browned.
  4. Transfer to a wire rack to cool, then refrigerate until thoroughly chilled, at least 1 hour. Garnish with whipped cream and lime slices before serving.

Notes

A homemade graham cracker crust is better if you have the time. The filling is naturally thick from the condensed milk reacting with the lime juice — don't overbake it.
