Belizean BBQ Fish

by Tino Gonzales, adapted by Sydney

Although this dish will never taste as good as it does on a secret beach after a long morning of fishing and sun, this at-home version can scratch the itch. Serve it with tortillas and a rum punch and it gets close.

Serves 4–6
Prep 20 min
Cook 40 min
Total 1 hr

Ingredients

  • For the Fish
  • 1½ lbs snapper
  • 3 roma tomatoes, cut into coins
  • 1 green bell pepper, cut into strips
  • 1 yellow onion, cut into strips
  • ¾ cup BBQ sauce
  • ½ cup butter
  • 1 lemon, juiced
  • 1 tbsp paprika
  • 2 tsp garlic powder
  • 1 tsp onion powder
  • 1 tsp annatto powder (chili powder is a fine substitute)
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • For the Potatoes
  • 2 lbs gold potatoes, cut into ¼-inch rounds
  • 4 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1½ tbsp paprika
  • ¼ cup butter
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Instructions

Potatoes

  1. Preheat the oven to 400°F.
  2. Wash and cut the potatoes into ¼-inch rounds. Place in an oven-safe baking dish.
  3. Mince the garlic and add to the potatoes. Add paprika, salt, and pepper.
  4. Mix everything with your hands until all potato pieces are evenly coated. Layer the rounds in the dish, one on top of another.
  5. Cube the butter and distribute evenly over the top.
  6. Bake for about 40 minutes, giving the potatoes a good mix at the halfway point.

Fish

  1. Preheat oven to 400°F (if not already). Wash and cut the tomatoes, onions, and peppers. Set aside.
  2. Melt the butter and whisk in the BBQ sauce, lemon juice, garlic powder, onion powder, and annatto powder.
  3. In a large baking dish, spread the onion and bell pepper strips across the bottom.
  4. Lay the snapper over the vegetables. Season with salt and pepper.
  5. Pour the butter and BBQ sauce mixture over everything, making sure each piece is covered.
  6. Layer tomato coins all over the top.
  7. Cover tightly with aluminum foil and bake for 20 minutes. Check for doneness before serving.

Notes

Serve the fish over the potatoes with tortillas on the side. Annatto powder can be hard to find — chili powder or ancho chili powder is a great substitute. For something closer to Tino's original, BBQ it: swap the baking dishes for aluminum trays covered in foil and cook on indirect heat for a similar amount of time. The potatoes will take longer than the fish.
